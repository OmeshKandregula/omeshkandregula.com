Summary

A structured peer review platform where getting feedback on your resume requires giving feedback first. Reciprocity as the engine. Structure as the product.

The Problem

Resume feedback is broken. Reddit threads get generic advice. Career centers are overloaded. Friends give vague reassurance. The problem isn't access to feedback — it's access to useful feedback from people who actually understand what you're applying for.

  • Unstructured feedback leads to surface-level comments, not actionable change.
  • No skin in the game — reviewers have zero incentive to go deep.
  • Best reviewers are peers in the same job search — not experts from above.
  • Free-form inputs produce noise, not signal.

Context

Founder & Product Lead Independent, 2025

Built from a personal frustration with job search feedback quality. Responsible for product design, engineering, and growth. Solo founder.

What I Built

A reciprocal review system with guided feedback structure.

  • Reciprocity gate: review one resume to unlock feedback on yours
  • Structured review form with section-level prompts for depth and specificity
  • Role-based matching — reviewed by peers in similar job tracks
  • Quality scoring and minimum word-count enforcement per section

Outcomes

88% review completion

Users who start a review finish it. Structure removes friction at every step.

+40% feedback quality

Section-level prompts produce longer, more specific, more actionable feedback.

50+ active users

Organic growth through word of mouth among job seekers at CMU and beyond.

Product Walkthrough

Key Decisions

Scope

Constraint over features

No AI suggestions. No infinite profiles. One clear value prop: structured peer critique, nothing more.

Access

Reciprocity over payment

Instead of charging money, we charge effort. You review someone else's resume to unlock feedback on yours.

UX

Structure over freedom

Opinionated forms beat blank text boxes. Prompting the reviewer produces better feedback every time.

Engineering

Speed over scalability

Simple stack, no premature optimization. Ship fast, learn fast. Scale is a problem worth having.

Key Takeaway

The best feedback systems don't pay reviewers — they make reviewing a prerequisite. Reciprocity creates quality without a single dollar spent on incentives.
