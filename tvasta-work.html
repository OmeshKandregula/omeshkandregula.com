Summary

Built a parameter control and monitoring system for Tvasta's large-format concrete 3D printer to improve dimensional accuracy and reduce material waste during construction-scale print runs.

The Problem

Large-format construction printing operates at the edge of what's controllable. Concrete is not a forgiving material. Slight deviations in extrusion rate, nozzle speed, or layer timing compound across meters of structure — producing waste, rework, or failed prints that cost far more than the materials alone.

  • Manual parameter tuning was inconsistent between operators and print runs.
  • No real-time feedback loop between sensor readings and print adjustments.
  • Dimensional errors only visible post-print — too late to correct mid-layer.

Context

Product Engineering Intern Tvasta, 2023

Tvasta builds large-format 3D printers for construction. Working at the intersection of hardware, software, and material science — at lab scale with production ambitions.

What I Built

Parameter control system with real-time monitoring and feedback.

  • Sensor integration for real-time extrusion rate and nozzle position tracking
  • Automated parameter adjustment logic based on deviation thresholds
  • Operator dashboard for live monitoring and manual override during runs
  • Print log analysis tooling for identifying root causes of dimensional errors

Outcomes

+20% dimensional accuracy

Measurable improvement in print precision across repeated layer runs.

-15% material waste

Fewer failed layers and corrective reprints — less concrete, less time lost.

Research-informed design

Constraints imposed by lab budget drove modular decisions that generalize better than over-engineered alternatives.

In Operation

Key Decisions

Architecture

Modularity over coupling

Sensor modules, control logic, and dashboards built as separate layers. Any part can be swapped without rebuilding the stack.

Priority

Research over throughput

Understanding why deviations happen matters more than printing faster. Instrumentation before optimization.

Constraints

Accepted the limits

Lab budget and materials defined the playing field. Working within real constraints produced more honest and durable solutions.

Validation

Real-world focus

No simulations. Every parameter change tested against an actual print. Physical results only.

Key Takeaway

In hardware, the feedback loop is physical. You can't mock a failed concrete layer. The discipline to instrument before optimizing — and to accept the constraints the material imposes — is what separates research from theater.
