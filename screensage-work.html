Project: ScreenSage
Product Concept

ScreenSage

A gamified approach to reducing phone screen time through social accountability and friendly competition.

Passive trackers fail because people don't respond to data about themselves. They respond to how they compare to people they care about.

Role Product Designer Student Build Sprint · IIT Madras
Outcome
-40%
Screen time across pilot participants ScreenSage · live pilot
Core Insight

"I don't care about the graph, I care if I beat my friend."

ScreenSage · user interview

The Problem

Awareness isn't enough.

Passive well-being tools show you a graph of your failure, then offer no reason to change.

"Time limit" notifications Ignored
Self-discipline Depletes daily
Blocking & limits Feel like punishment

Passive tools · observed failure modes

The Approach

Turning a personal habit into a shared challenge.

"Limit yourself."

→"Beat your friends."

Squads of 3 to 5 Weekly reset Lowest time wins

Weekly Leagues

3 to 5 friends. Resets every Monday.

Cumulative Scoring

Total screen time ranks the squad. Lowest wins, live.

Discovery

"Don't ask them what they want."

Jobs-to-be-Done interviews: No pitching, just their last week with the phone.

Question asked

"Tell me about the last time you felt you spent too much time on your phone."

Insight Uncovered

Doom-scrolling spiked while waiting or feeling lonely. The missing element was social.

What the research overturned

Global leaderboards

→Squads of people you know. Your roommate matters, strangers don't.

Infinite streaks

→Weekly resets. A bad day shouldn't end the game.

Locking apps

→Positive competition. Locks drove uninstalls, play drove engagement.

Learnings & Reflection

Talk Less. Observe More.

Real behavior surfaced more than stated preferences ever did.

Psychology over Features

Behavior change is a motivation problem, not a feature problem.

Validate Early

Proving the core mechanism beat building a full feature suite.

Iterate on Friction

Cutting onboarding to one step, "Join a Squad," lifted conversion roughly 40%.
