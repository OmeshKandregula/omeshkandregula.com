Summary

ScreenSage turns screen time into a shared social signal — not a private shame metric. Small, closed squads see each other's usage and hold each other accountable without punishment mechanics or nanny-mode restrictions.

The Problem

Screen time apps don't work. App limits get disabled. Weekly reports are ignored in private. Shame without stakes changes nothing. The research on behavior change is clear: social accountability outperforms self-tracking by a wide margin — but no one has applied it to screen time in a way that doesn't feel dystopian.

  • Self-tracked data lives in a vacuum — no one else sees it, no consequences.
  • Hard limits breed resentment. People disable them immediately.
  • No product has cracked low-friction social accountability for this problem.
  • Open leaderboards create anxiety, not motivation — small trusted groups are the answer.

Context

Founder & Product Lead Independent, 2025

Built after recognizing that every screen time solution punishes rather than motivates. Behavioral design applied to a genuinely hard consumer habit problem. Invite-only beta with handpicked early squads.

What I Built

Squad-based screen time sharing with soft accountability mechanics.

  • Squad system: closed groups of 3-6 people who opt into seeing each other's usage
  • Weekly digest shared within the squad — not a public leaderboard
  • Awareness-first design: no hard blocks, no app locks, no punishments
  • Nudge engine: gentle prompts when usage exceeds personal baseline by 20%+

Outcomes

40% screen time reduction

Average across early beta users over 4 weeks. Awareness alone moves the needle.

Zero disabled limits

No one has turned the app off. Soft accountability outperforms hard locks in retention.

Invite-only growth

Each squad member invites their own group. Organic, trust-based expansion from day one.

Key Decisions

Core Mechanic

Social over self-tracking

Private dashboards change nothing. Shared visibility with people you trust changes behavior.

Tone

Play over punishment

The goal is awareness, not shame. No red warnings. No blocked apps. The squad is the nudge.

Distribution

Invite-only over global

Trust is the product. Open signups would destroy the dynamic that makes squads work. Scarcity is intentional.

Research

Observation over asking

Users don't know why they pick up their phones. Behavioral patterns from actual usage data beat survey responses every time.

Key Takeaway

Awareness shared between people you respect is more powerful than any app lock. The secret isn't restriction — it's making your behavior visible to the three people whose opinion of you actually matters.
